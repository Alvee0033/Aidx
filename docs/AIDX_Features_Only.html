AIDX Medical Assistant: Features and Technical Report

Abstract

AIDX is a cross‑platform mHealth companion that unifies AI‑assisted symptom analysis (with report analyzer and Medline cross‑checks), wearable vitals, medication reminders with prescription scanning, emergency SOS with auto‑dispatch and fall detection, and community support. The app emphasizes concise guidance, privacy‑respecting defaults, and graceful fallbacks when permissions or connectivity are limited.

Problem Statement

When you feel unwell, the real question is simple: “Is this serious, and what should I do now?” Most people bounce between search results, forums, a wearable app, a separate reminder app, and maps for nearby care—losing time and confidence along the way. First and foremost, users need rapid, trustworthy Symptom Analysis AI that gives clear next steps in plain language. They also need that guidance connected to the rest of their health life: live vitals from their watch, easy prescription scanning to set reminders, quick access to hospitals or pharmacies, and one‑tap SOS with fall detection when things look urgent. AIDX brings these parts together so help is fast, consistent, and actionable—reducing anxiety, missed doses, and delays in getting the right care.

1. Introduction

  • Purpose: AIDX is a cross‑platform mHealth companion that combines AI guidance, wearable vitals, medication adherence, emergency support, and community features into one coherent experience.
  • Scope: Everyday health tasks—triage of new symptoms, understanding medications, staying on schedule, monitoring vitals, and accessing care quickly when needed.
  • Audience: Health‑conscious individuals, caregivers, and early adopters of wearables seeking actionable, privacy‑respecting tools.
  • Differentiators: Medical‑only AI prompts, optional inclusion of live vitals in analyses, auto‑SOS with fall detection, and a shareable Health ID for emergencies.
  • Constraints: Designed for real‑world use—works with permission fallbacks, shows clear offline states, and keeps guidance concise to avoid overload.

2. Features (Dashboard Order)

1) AI Symptoms Analysis

  • Overview: Guides users through symptom entry and returns concise analysis with likely conditions, severity, and clear next steps.
  • Inputs: Text description, intensity (mild/moderate/severe), duration, optional age/gender, optional photo.
  • Smart context: Option to auto‑fill demographics from Health ID and include latest wearable vitals for richer triage.
  • Outputs: Short assessment plus practical self‑care measures or escalation cues when appropriate.
  • Medline cross‑check: When available, AI findings are cross‑checked against trusted Medline/MedlinePlus references to reinforce accuracy and provide reputable reading links.
  • Sub‑feature – Report Analyzer: Accepts a brief description and/or a photo of a medical report (e.g., ECG, lab sheet), validates image size/format, and summarizes key findings in plain language.
  • History: Saves prior analyses for quick reference and trend awareness.
  • User flow: Describe symptoms → optionally attach image → toggle vitals/Health ID → get concise guidance → save or share.
  • Notes: Prompts stay medical‑only; if confidence is low, guidance is conservative and encourages professional follow‑up.

2) AI Voice Chat

  • Overview: Medical‑only conversational assistant for quick questions about symptoms, prevention, and self‑care.
  • Tone: Short, clear, and practical responses focused on safe, actionable guidance.
  • Typical asks: Headache concerns, sprain care, fasting prep, when to see a clinician.
  • Privacy: Designed to avoid collecting unnecessary data; avoid sharing sensitive identifiers in free‑text.

3) Drug Info

  • Overview: Quick lookups for medications—uses, common side effects, and key precautions.
  • Value: Helps users understand prescriptions and set expectations for typical reactions.
  • Notes: Not a substitute for clinician dosing; always follow your prescriber’s instructions.

4) Hospital Finder

  • Overview: Nearby hospitals/ERs with distance, directions, and contact options.
  • Key actions: One‑tap navigation and call to facility.
  • Permissions: Requires location permission for accurate results; falls back to manual search if denied.

5) Doctor & Pharmacy

  • Overview: Lists clinics, practitioners, and pharmacies close by.
  • Key actions: Call, navigate, review basic availability when provided.
  • Notes: Filters differ by region; availability depends on provider data sources.

6) Blood Donation

  • Overview: Donation basics, eligibility reminders, and pointers to nearby drives/centers.
  • Live GPS & City: Auto‑detects current location to suggest nearby donation points; users can also select a city manually if GPS is off.
  • Blood Group Selection: Filter or post requests by blood group (e.g., A+, O‑, AB+), making matching faster and more accurate.
  • Direct Messages (DM): Allow requesters and donors to connect via in‑app DM or configured channels (e.g., phone/Telegram) with opt‑in contact sharing.
  • Extras: Share campaigns and review preparation tips.
  • Safety: Promotes official guidelines; defers medical eligibility questions to certified centers.

7) Medication Reminder

  • Overview: Schedules alerts and prompts for intake confirmation to support adherence.
  • Flow: Add medicine/time → receive notification → mark taken → optional history; supports snooze and missed‑dose catch‑up.
  • Prescription Scan: Capture or upload a prescription; OCR extracts medicine names, strengths, and frequencies to pre‑fill reminders for user review before saving.
  • Quiet hours: Optional setting to suppress non‑critical alerts during sleep windows.

8) Medical Timeline

  • Overview: Chronological view of vitals, reminders, symptom analyses, and notes.
  • Filters: Show/hide event types; jump to dates; highlight anomalies.
  • Benefits: Gives context across days/weeks for better decisions and follow‑ups.

9) Wearable Tracker

  • Overview: Connects to compatible smartwatches to display live heart rate, SpO₂, BP (where available), temperature, and steps.
  • Experience: Scan → connect → see vitals and connection status; auto‑reconnect reduces drop‑offs.
  • Battery: Shows watch battery where available; gentle polling to conserve power.

10) Emergency SOS

  • Overview: One‑tap access to emergency help; can warn when vitals are consistently outside safe bounds.
  • Actions: Trigger SOS, see local emergency number, optionally notify contacts; cancel within a short countdown to prevent false alarms.
  • Thresholds: Monitors HR and SpO₂ against safe limits over a sustained window before escalating (e.g., HR > 180 BPM or SpO₂ < 85% for ~30s).
  • Auto‑SOS (Vitals): When abnormal vitals persist past the timer, the app starts an SOS countdown and can auto‑dispatch if the user does not cancel.
  • Fall Detection: Integrates with device sensors and wearables to detect strong impacts and immobility; prompts the user and can auto‑start SOS if unresponsive.
  • Location & share: Optionally attach current location and Health ID summary when contacting trusted numbers or channels.

11) Health ID

  • Overview: Compact, shareable health profile; optional QR for quick access in urgent situations.
  • Contents: Identity, allergies, conditions, emergency contacts, and other essentials.
  • Control: User chooses what to include/exclude before sharing; QR can be regenerated.

12) Health Habits

  • Overview: Tracks daily wellness habits (hydration, movement, mindfulness, sleep proxies).
  • Motivation: Streaks, simple badges, and gentle nudges to build consistency.
  • Flexibility: Editable targets and reminders; skip days without breaking long‑term progress visibility.

13) Sleep & Fall Detect

  • Overview: Highlights sleep patterns and potential fall events using sensors and optional wearable signals.
  • Value: Increases awareness of rest quality and personal safety incidents.
  • Notes: False positives are possible; users can dismiss/confirm events to improve future sensitivity.

14) Community Support

  • Overview: Safe space for peer support—share experiences, tips, and encouragement.
  • Benefits: Social reinforcement and lived‑experience insights.

3. Technology Used

  • Frontend: Flutter (Dart) with Material components, custom theme, responsive layouts; Times New Roman for print‑ready docs.
  • Backend/Cloud: Firebase Auth, Firestore (documents/collections), Cloud Storage; optional indices initialization utilities.
  • AI & ML: Custom LLM API integration for chat, symptom and report analysis; Google ML Kit Text Recognition for OCR of prescriptions/reports.
  • Wearables & Sensors: BLE smartwatch services (Heart Rate, SpO₂, BP, Battery, Thermometer, Fitness); platform channel for live vitals; device sensors via sensors_plus.
  • Media & Realtime: Camera/image picker for capture/upload; WebRTC scaffolding for AI video calls (signaling backend pluggable).
  • Notifications & Background: Firebase Cloud Messaging + flutter_local_notifications; background service hooks for monitoring/alerts.
  • Utilities: Provider state management, shared_preferences, url_launcher, qr_flutter, syncfusion charts (for trends).

4. Architecture Overview

  • UI Layer: Screens and widgets for dashboard, features, and dialogs; Provider for lightweight state.
  • Services Layer: AI (LLM API), Wearable (BLE + platform channel), Firebase (Auth/DB), Notifications, Background, Places.
  • Data Layer: Firestore collections (users, health_data, medications, reminders, symptoms, reports, community); indices/seed docs to stabilize queries.
  • Data flow: Device/user input → services → provider state → UI; optional persistence to Firestore and history views.

5. Security & Privacy

  • Authentication: Firebase Auth with per‑user access in Firestore via rules.
  • Least data: Only the minimum needed for each task; explicit permission gates for camera, Bluetooth, notifications, location.
  • Secrets hygiene: Prefer runtime configuration or secure storage; avoid hard‑coding API keys.
  • User control: Share settings for Health ID; quiet hours for reminders; SOS countdown to prevent false dispatches.

6. Limitations & Risks

  • Device variability: BLE characteristics and performance differ across watches; provide graceful fallbacks.
  • AI uncertainty: Model outputs can be imperfect; design emphasizes brevity, caution, and encouragement to seek care when unsure.
  • Permissions: Features depend on user consent; manual flows should remain available when permissions are denied.
  • Connectivity: Some features need internet (AI, maps, cloud); show clear offline states and retries.

7. Roadmap

  • Signaling backend: Production WebRTC signaling for AI video calls and live annotations.
  • Deeper medication intelligence: Interaction checks, duplication warnings, refill reminders.
  • Expanded wearables: Broader device matrices, proprietary characteristics mapping, better battery optimization.
  • On‑device ML: More inference at the edge for privacy and low‑latency use cases.

8. Conclusion

  • Summary: AIDX unifies AI guidance, wearable vitals, reminders, and community into a single, privacy‑respecting mHealth experience.
  • Impact: Faster triage, better adherence, clearer context for decisions, and safer escalation when needed.

11. User Personas & Use Cases

  • Persona A – Health‑Active Adult: Uses wearables; wants fast triage for minor issues and adherence support.
  • Persona B – Caregiver: Monitors dependents’ vitals and medication schedules; needs clear escalation paths.
  • Persona C – Volunteer Donor: Seeks donation drives and responds to blood type requests; prefers in‑app DMs.
  • Use cases: report a new symptom; scan a prescription to create reminders; connect a smartwatch; receive auto‑SOS after a detected fall; find a nearby ER; share Health ID via QR.

13. Evaluation Plan

  • Usability tests: Task‑completion on symptom triage, reminder creation (incl. prescription scan), and SOS simulation.
  • Metrics: Time‑to‑guidance, adherence rate, false‑positive rate for falls/SOS, NPS for clarity of advice.
  • Pilot: 2–4 week pilot with weekly check‑ins; collect qualitative feedback and telemetry (opt‑in).

14. Accessibility & Inclusion

  • Readability: Large fonts, high‑contrast themes, consistent iconography.
  • Voice & language: Support for TTS; plan for localization of key screens and alerts.
  • Network & devices: Works on low‑end phones; offline‑tolerant where feasible (local reminders).

15. Ethics & Safety

  • Scope: AI is informational—not diagnostic; encourages clinician follow‑up for uncertainty or red flags.
  • Auto‑SOS: Clear countdown and cancel affordance; thresholds documented; logs for review.
  • Data: Minimal necessary collection; user‑controlled sharing (e.g., Health ID, location).

16. Deployment & Operations

  • Environments: Dev/staging/prod Firebase projects; secret management via runtime config.
  • Monitoring: Crash/error logging, push delivery stats, BLE connection health.
  • Releases: CI to build artifacts; staged rollouts; change‑logs and rollback plan.
